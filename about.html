The Test

Background of Trinity

Trinity was the first detonation of a nuclear bomb, the test was conducted by the United States Army, as a part of the Manhattan Project, and directed by J. Robert Oppenheimer. The test site was decided to be the Jornada del Muerto Desert, in New Mexico.

The Countdown to Zero

On July 16, 1945- with 425 people present, the world would witness the first detonation of a nuclear weapon. Strapped to the top of a 100-foot tower, the bomb- which was informally named the "Gadget", and later become the "Fat Man" was dropped. At 5:29 am, the biggest explosion would violently grow into a mushroom-shaped cloud. This atmospheric test- powered by a plutonium implosion fission device- would release 25 kilotons of TNT (or 100 TJ of energy) and be seen up to 160 miles away.

After the Clouds Dissipate

After the explosion faded away, both shock and euphoria enveloped the scientists, as "the atom bomb did not fit into any preconceptions possessed by anybody." -Norris Bradbury. Less than a month later, the bomb would be dropped and detonated 1,650 feet over Nagasaki, leading to around 40,000 people died instantly, and by January 1946, the death toll was now around 70,000.
